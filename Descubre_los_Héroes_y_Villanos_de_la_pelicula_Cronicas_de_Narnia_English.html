THE LION, THE WITCH AND THE WARDROBE

Welcome to Narnia, a magical world where bravery and friendship are essential in the fight between good and evil! Join us as we discover the Pevensie siblings: Peter, the determined leader; Susan, the pragmatic warrior; Edmund, in search of redemption; and Lucy, the innocent dreamer, along with the powerful lion Aslan, a symbol of hope, and the evil Queen Jadis, who has plunged Narnia into eternal winter. Together, these characters will guide us on an unforgettable journey full of challenges and lessons about love and loyalty.

ASLAN

Aslan is one of the most iconic characters from The Chronicles of Narnia: The Lion, the Witch and the Wardrobe. He is portrayed as a majestic lion with a golden mane that shines like the sun, reflecting his status as King of Narnia. He represents authority, justice and compassion, acting as a guide and protector to the Pevensie siblings, especially Lucy, who feels a special connection to him.

Through his wisdom and power, Aslan inspires the characters to do what is right and fight against the oppression of the White Witch. His death at the hands of the White Witch is a pivotal moment that symbolizes sacrifice for the sake of others. However, his resurrection proves that good always triumphs over evil, reinforcing the series' themes of hope and redemption. Aslan embodies values ​​of love and justice, becoming a timeless symbol of what it means to be a true leader and protector.



Lucy Pevensie

The youngest of the Pevensie siblings, she is innocent and brave. Her discovery of Narnia makes her a bridge between the human and magical worlds, and her unwavering faith in Aslan makes her pivotal to the story.

Edmund Pevensie

Initially traitorous, his inner conflict represents the struggle between good and evil. Throughout the story, he experiences a journey of redemption, showing that forgiveness and change are possible.

Susan Pevensie

The eldest sister, who acts as the voice of reason and protector of her family. Her more practical approach contrasts with Lucy's faith, bringing balance to the group.

Peter Pevensie

The natural leader of the siblings, his bravery and nobility are essential to facing the challenges in Narnia. His personal growth reflects the importance of responsibility and sacrifice.

The White Witch (Jadis)

The main antagonist, representing absolute evil. Her dominance over Narnia and desire for power contrast with the ideals of Aslan and the protagonists, creating a central conflict in the story.

Mr. Tumnus

A faun who personifies kindness and friendship. His relationship with Lucy and their conflict between following orders and doing the right thing highlight the themes of loyalty and redemption in Narnia"
